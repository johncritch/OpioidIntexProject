The Epidemic


"We still prescribe over 80 percent of the world’s opioids to less than five percent of the
world’s population. So, we still have an over-prescribing epidemic"


How Can We Be Part of the Solution?


Health Care Professionals

1) Opioids do not need to be the first-line of action for chronic pain.

2) Establish and measure goals for pain with patients if opioid are the course of action.

3) Discuss benefits, risks, and availability of nonopioid therapies with patients. Show statistics of the opioid epidiemic, the negative side effects, and what opioid misuse looks like.

Government Officials

1) Advertise free opioid addiction and misuse help, and provide overdose assistance.

2) Increase awareness of opioid overdose reversal drugs to the public, such as naxolone. Increase availability to the public for free.

3) Provide incentives for cities and schools to increase awareness of what opioids are, the harmful effects of them, and the growing epidemic.



The 5 Point Strategy To Combat the Opioid Crisis

                

1. Better Data by Strengthening Public Health Surveillance



2. Better Pain Management





3. Improving access to treatments, prevention, recovery services

4. Increasing availability of overdose-reversing drugs



5. Supporting cutting edge opioid research
